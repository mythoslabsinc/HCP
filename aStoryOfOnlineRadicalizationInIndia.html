A Story of Online Radicalization in India


The National Investigation Agency (NIA) has looked into over 60 social media accounts connected to a previously banned group. These accounts were allegedly spreading hate and targeting the Indian government and judiciary.

The analysis of these accounts revealed posts and videos with inflammatory content against the government and the judiciary. The group had a presence in many states before being banned in September 2022.

The NIA claims that this group was planning to create a militia and generate resources for a campaign against the Indian government to establish an "Islamic caliphate." They had a hierarchical structure with units at various levels.

The NIA also alleges that the group had a hidden agenda outlined in a document titled "India 2047: Towards Rule of Islam in India," which was seized during an investigation.

The group's strategy involved training its members in weapons, using violence selectively, causing communal disharmony, and infiltrating key institutions like the police, army, and judiciary.

The NIA suggests that the group had a hit squad for eliminating targets identified by the organization, and it was involved in several violent crimes.

The group had alleged links to other banned organizations, particularly the Students Islamic Movement of India (SIMI).

Additionally, the NIA claims that the group played a role in organizing and financing protests against the Citizenship (Amendment) Act and the National Register of Citizens.

In summary, the NIA has investigated the activities of a previously banned group with a history of violence and alleged extremist objectives.
