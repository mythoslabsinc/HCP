What is Harmful Content?

Harmful content online that tries to trick you into hating others or particular groups is one of the main problems that Indian internet users face across the nation. It's important to comprehend what all of this means and how it might affect you and our culture.

False information, provocative language, and skewed narratives are common elements of harmful content that aims to weaponize your emotions. It's intended to take advantage of your weaknesses and incite hostility towards particular people or groups.

This content might take the shape of divisive narratives that target various religious or racial communities in India, propagate stereotypes, or encourage intolerance. It seeks to incite deep-seated hostility, prejudice, and discrimination among us.

It's important for you as an Indian internet user to be aware of these tactics and understand all the ways harmful content creators online can reach and affect you.

5 Ways Bad Actors Reach You

Personalized targeting

Bad actors collect information about you to tailor their content specifically for you, based on your interests, affiliations, or friends.

False narratives and misinformation

They create or spread false information, conspiracy theories, or distorted narratives that manipulate your beliefs and emotions.

Amplification through algorithms

Bad actors understand how social media algorithms work and use them to ensure their content reaches more people, leveraging engagement to gain more visibility.

Online communities and echo chambers

They create or join online communities where they share their ideologies and reinforce their beliefs, limiting exposure to diverse perspectives.

Exploiting emotions and vulnerabilities

Bad actors use emotional triggers, such as fear, anger, or injustice, to manipulate your feelings and rally you towards their cause.
